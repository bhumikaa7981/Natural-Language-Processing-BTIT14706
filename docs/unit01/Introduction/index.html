The actual definition

Natural Language Processing is the branch of AI that lets computers understand, interpret, process, and generate human language — the way you understood "bro, I'm dead" instantly, and a computer has to be painstakingly taught to.

Bonus round · Why language is hard for machines

Three sentences that break computers (and delight linguists).

"I never said she stole my money."
Tap a word to stress it — the whole sentence's meaning shifts. Seven words, seven completely different accusations.
"The chicken is ready to eat."
The chicken (already cooked) is ready for us to eat.
"Time flies like an arrow."
"Fruit flies like a banana."
Time (the concept) speeds by, the way an arrow does. A poetic comparison.
Part 5 · Current challenges · 10 min

Machines still lose to a single raised eyebrow.

🙃

Sarcasm

"Wow. Great work." Praise, or the opposite? Depends entirely on tone the text doesn't carry.

🍳

Memes & Slang

"Bro is cooked." Nobody is cooking. Slang shifts meaning faster than any model can be retrained.

🔥

Emoji

Hot? Awesome? Trending? One emoji, three completely different intents, all contextual.
