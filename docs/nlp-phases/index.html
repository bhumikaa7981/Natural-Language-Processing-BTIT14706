Ambiguity's three disguises

Language didn't evolve for computers. It evolved for us — full of shortcuts, sarcasm and slang. Here's the lineup.

A

Ambiguity

"Visiting relatives can be annoying." Are the relatives annoying, or is the visiting?

S

Synonymy

"big", "large", "huge" — different words, overlapping meaning. Machines have to know they're cousins.

H

Homonymy

"bat" (animal) vs "bat" (cricket gear) — identical spelling, zero relation.

💡 One big idea to remember

NLP isn't one algorithm. It's a pipeline of stages, each resolving a little more ambiguity than the last — until raw text becomes usable meaning.
